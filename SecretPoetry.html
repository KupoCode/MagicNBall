Dusting Off the Moon

The moon is just silly, it really, truly is.
Now hear me out, I’ve been as enchanted as you with her,
I’ve heard the songs, I’ve seen the paintings.

But what is it really?
A worthless rock, in any other place, at any other time.
It’s not a special hue; it’s a dull, lifeless orb circling us,
like a cosmic clock just reminding us it’s bedtime.

It’s not without its merits: the tides, it’s pull keeping us in check…
But that’s not why we gaze, now is it? What keeps our eyes up high at night then?
What makes it wondrous and terrifying all at once,
when the starry sky lies besides her, also calling to us?

The moon is no counterpart to the sun, yet we praise them both as equals.
It provides no warmth, no light of its own, yet it is the poet’s darling.

It’s glow a facade, only by chance do we love it and fear it and respect it.
It’s for ‘those who are different’, yet it shows up daily.

The Moon is undeserving, but maybe that’s just me…

---
But maybe, that’s just me.

I am the moon, on your sights by cosmic luck.
I am the product of happenstance, of chance, of probability.
Glowing merely as a reflection of others.
Useful because it’s what I happened to be.
Always right at the place, right at the time.
Not by choice, but because the heavens dictate it so.

I am loved and cherished and adored,
when there’s millions of other rocks out there
With the same materials and the same structures just like me.
But they quite can’t be, because they’re not in the exact ‘when’ like me.

Look up at night and see all there is to it.
Some undeserving dull rock, mundane and in the way of the stars,
or tonight’s celestial main event always returning by popular demand.
I am both at once and neither one, I guess I’m just as silly.
I really, truly am.
